        <h2>Important Moments in 2015</h2>
        <ul id= "moments">
        <li>November 4, 2014: Joe Maddon is hired as the manager of the Chicago Cubs. He has a great reputation for getting the most out of his players.</li>
        <li>December 16, 2014: Jon Lester is signed for $155 million as the ace of the Cubs, who hope to contend in 2015</li>
            <li>April 12, 2015: In the ninth inning trailing 4-5, Dexter Fowler hits a 2-run homerun and the Cubs win 6-5. Even many casual fans began to take notice as the Cubs start the season on a winning pace. </li>
            <li>April 17, 2015: Hyped top prospect Kris Bryant makes his major league debut at Wrigley Field. He is the first in a wave of young talent that will contribute to the Cubs in 2015
            <li>April 21, 2015: Addison Russell also makes his major league debut, and the Cubs scored 5 runs late to cap a 9-8 victory in Pittsburgh.</li>
            <li>May 11-16, 2015: The Cubs win 6 games in a row, one of the earliest signs that the Cubs are emerging as a contender.</li>
            <li>June 17, 2015: The Cubs explode for 17 runs and shut out the Indians. Hyped rookie Kyle Schwarber made his MLB debut and hit a homer. Kris Bryant had a grand slam.</li>
            <li>July 14, 2015: Kris Bryant and Anthony Rizzo were selected as All-Stars as well as Home Run Derby participants in Cincinnati.
            <li>July 25, 2015: The Cubs get no hit for the first time in 50 years by Philadelphia's Cole Hamels at Wrigley Field.</li>
            <li>July 27, 2015: Kris Bryant hits a walkoff homer with the Cubs trailing in the ninth. It is the first walkoff homerun with the Cubs trailing since 2007, when they won their division. </li>
            <li>August 6-9, 2015: The Cubs swept the Giants at Wrigley Field, taking the lead for the second  NL wild card spot. They would go on a 9-game winning streak after that, and win 19 games overall in August.
            <li>August 30, 2015: Jake Arrieta pitches a no-hitter against the Dodgers in Los Angeles. It is the first Cubs no-no since 2008.</li>
            <li> September 7, 2015: The Cubs beat the Cardinals 9-0, proving that the Cubs can contend with the powerhouse Cards. </li>
            <li>September 19, 2015: Cubs beat the Cardinals at Wrigley Field (I was there!)</li>
            <li> September 26, 2015: The Cubs clinch a wild cardspot, their first postseason appearence since 2008</li>
        </ul>
